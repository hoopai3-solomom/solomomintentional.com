Step 2: Ovarian Stimulation

For about 10-14 days, you'll take hormone injections to stimulate your ovaries to produce multiple eggs (instead of the single egg your body typically releases each cycle).

What to expect during this phase:

  • Some bloating and mild discomfort as your ovaries enlarge
  • Emotional fluctuations from the hormones

Step 3: Egg Retrieval

Once your eggs are ready, you'll undergo a short outpatient procedure:

  • The retrieval takes about 15-20 minutes
  • You'll be under light sedation (you won't feel anything)
  • Your doctor uses an ultrasound-guided needle to collect the eggs
  • Most women go home within an hour and rest for a day or two

The Emotional Side Is Real

Even if you're confident in your decision, you might experience:

  • Anxiety about whether it will "work"
  • Grief about not having a partner to do this with

Your Body Will Need Recovery Time

Most women bounce back quickly, but give yourself grace:

  • Take a few days off work if possible
  • Avoid intense exercise for 1-2 weeks
  • Stay hydrated and rest
  • Some bloating may last a week or more

When Is the Right Time to Freeze Your Eggs?

The short answer: sooner is generally better from a biological standpoint.

Egg quality and quantity decline with age, with the most significant drop occurring after 35. However, "the right time" is also about:

  • Your emotional readiness
  • Your financial situation
  • Your life circumstances

If you're thinking about it, don't wait years to start the conversation. Even getting your fertility tested gives you valuable information.
